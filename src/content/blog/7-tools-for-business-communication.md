Communication is not as easy as it seems when done on a professional level. Poor communication can not only harm employee and customer relationships but the entire business as well. Regardless of your business size, poor communication can't be tolerated and should always be avoided.

Initially, you need to learn the real importance of effective business communication. Afterward, you need to put your efforts into improving your team. Modern-day technology has made many communication tools available that you can use for improving communication inside your business.

**Key Insight:** 72% of business leaders believe that their team productivity has improved due to improved communication.

Let's explore seven powerful tools that can transform how your business communicates:

## 1. Shout.com - Best for surveys

Privacy-focused and cookie-free surveys that revolutionize feedback collection, lead generation, and audience communication. Shout.com is GDPR and CCPA compliant, making it ideal for businesses that prioritize data protection.

### Key Features:
- Create Surveys, Quizzes, Forms & Calculators
- Question Logic
- Text / Answer Piping
- Single and Multi Question Per Page Surveys
- Theme Customization & Branding
- Over 20 Question Types
- Net Promoter Score
- Filter and Compare Data Sets (Reports)
- Pseudonymization
- Custom Survey Languages
- Redirect to External URL on Submission

**Pricing:** Free trial available, Pro plan $25/month (paid annually)

## 2. Freshworks - Best help desk tool

Help desk software used by more than 60,000 businesses worldwide. Freshworks streamlines customer support and internal communication through its comprehensive platform.

### Key Features:
- **Omnichannel support:** Streamlines tickets for multiple communication channels
- **Collaboration:** Improves team collaboration by keeping agents informed
- **Automation:** Reduces manual effort from agents
- **Reporting and analytics:** Analyze performance metrics

**Pricing:** Free to use, Enterprise plan $79 per agent per month (billed annually)

## 3. Happy Scribe - Best for internal and external communication

Transcription services tool that simplifies communication processes by converting audio and video content into text. Support for 60+ languages with both AI and human transcription options.

### Accuracy Options:
- **AI transcription:** 85% accuracy, completed in 5 minutes
- **Human transcription:** 99% accuracy, completed in 24 hours

### Key Features:
- Machine translation
- Security & Confidentiality
- APIs & integrations
- No limits on uploads
- Import from other platforms

**Pricing:** Free plan available, Business plan $29/month (billed annually)

## 4. Slack - Best for instant messaging

Popular communication tool providing real-time communication, video conferencing, instant chat, and project management capabilities. Slack has become the go-to platform for modern workplace communication.

### Key Features:
- Real-time employee internal communication
- Public and private channels
- Ability to integrate third-party programs
- File sharing and collaboration
- Search functionality across all conversations

**Pricing:** Free version (one workspace, 90 days message history), Business plan $12.50/month

## 5. Zoom - Best for video communication

Video conferencing tool that has replaced phone systems for many remote companies. Zoom became essential during the remote work revolution and continues to be a leader in video communication.

### Key Features:
- Video calls for internal and external communication
- Option for recording meetings for later use
- Screen sharing and chat features
- Option for setting up separate conference rooms for team-building exercises
- Breakout rooms for smaller group discussions

**Pricing:** Free to use, Business plan $219.90/month (annual payment offers $43 discount)

## 6. SharePoint - Best for document collaboration and intranet

Promotes efficiency in organizations for synchronizing file sharing, news, information, and resources. SharePoint serves as a central hub for all organizational content and communication.

### Key Features:
- Create document libraries, pages, and lists on intranet websites
- Sync and store files in the cloud
- Use workflows and lists to manage daily activities
- Integration with Microsoft Office suite
- Version control and document management

**Pricing:** $5 per user per month with annual subscription (integrated with Microsoft Suite)

## 7. Microsoft Teams - Best for video chat

Similar to Zoom but with advanced chat features and unique "together mode" that creates a more engaging virtual meeting experience.

### Key Features:
- Easy integrations with Microsoft apps
- One channel that compensates for all communication forms
- User-friendly and simple to set up
- Together mode for immersive meetings
- Persistent chat alongside video calls

**Pricing:** Essentials $4 per user per month, Advanced plans up to $12.50 per user per month

## Choosing the Right Communication Tools

When selecting communication tools for your business, consider:

1. **Team Size:** Some tools scale better for larger organizations
2. **Budget:** Factor in per-user costs as your team grows
3. **Integration Needs:** Choose tools that work well with your existing software
4. **Security Requirements:** Ensure compliance with your industry standards
5. **User Experience:** Tools should be intuitive for all team members

## The Impact of Better Communication

Implementing the right communication tools can lead to:

- **Increased Productivity:** Teams work more efficiently with clear communication channels
- **Better Customer Service:** Streamlined support processes improve customer satisfaction
- **Enhanced Collaboration:** Cross-functional teams can work together more effectively
- **Reduced Miscommunication:** Clear channels prevent costly misunderstandings
- **Improved Remote Work:** Distributed teams can stay connected and aligned

## Conclusion

No business can be successful without effective communication. While creating an ideal communication strategy can be challenging, these software tools are designed to make communication easier for organizations.

The key is to evaluate your specific needs and choose tools that complement each other rather than overlap. Start with one or two core platforms and gradually expand your communication toolkit as your business grows.

Remember, the best communication tool is the one your team actually uses. Invest time in training and adoption to ensure these tools deliver their full potential for your organization.
